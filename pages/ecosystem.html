Zebra Jumping Spider (Salticus scenicus)

Native to Europe, this species has spread widely across North America, including Ontario, likely through accidental human-mediated transport such as shipping or commerce.

Asiatic Wall Jumping Spider (Attulus fasciger)

Originally from Asia, this spider is thought to have arrived in North America via shipping containers and has since been observed in urban areas of Ontario.

Myrmarachne formicaria

Native to Europe and Asia, this ant-mimic jumping spider was first detected in North America in the 2000s. Likely introduced via horticultural or cargo shipments.

Attulus ammophilus

Known from parts of Europe, this species has been recorded in Ontario, possibly introduced through plant trade or soil transport.

Palaearctic Antmimic Jumping Spider (Synageles venator)

A Eurasian species, it mimics ants to avoid predation. Reports suggest it may have entered Ontario through global trade.

Pantropical Jumping Spider (Plexippus paykulli)

Found throughout the tropics, this spider thrives in human-made environments and is spreading in Ontario, likely through human transportation networks.

Adanson's House Jumper (Hasarius adansoni)

A pantropical spider introduced through commerce. It is known for living in and around buildings and is occasionally sighted in Ontario.

Black-palped Jumping Spider (Pseudeuophrys erratica)

Originally from Eurasia, this species was detected in North America via ports and has been recorded in Ontario environments.
