Welcome to Ontario's Jumping Spider Community

Ontario is home to an incredible diversity of jumping spiders (Salticidae), each with unique behaviors and adaptations. These tiny acrobats of the arachnid world captivate with their intelligence, vibrant colors, and remarkable hunting skills.

What makes jumping spiders special? Their exceptional vision (with eyes that can see in nearly 360 degrees), complex courtship dances, and ability to leap up to 50 times their body length set them apart from other spiders.

Whether you're a curious beginner or an experienced arachnophile, our resources will help you:

  • Identify local species through our visual guide
  • Understand their ecological importance
  • Learn safe observation techniques
  • Contribute to citizen science projects

Jumping Spider Facts & Tips

  • 🕷️ They don't spin webs to catch prey instead, they actively hunt using excellent vision.
  • 🦶 They don't walk like typical spiders instead of the unsettling clumsy glide many spiders have, jumping spiders move in short, deliberate, almost robotic steps, which many people find less creepy.
  • 👀 They have incredible eyesight — 8 eyes total, with two large front-facing eyes that provide sharp, binocular vision. Unlike most spiders (which have 6 forward-facing eyes), jumping spiders have only 4 facing forward. The other 4 are spaced along the sides and back of the head, giving them nearly 360° awareness. Their large central eyes also give them a surprisingly expressive, almost human look.
  • 🐜 They can jump up to 50 times their body length using hydraulic pressure in their legs.
  • 🌍 Found worldwide over 6,000 species in diverse environments.
  • 🧠 Surprisingly curious they can learn, remember, and even recognize human faces.
  • 😌 Very shy and rarely bite their venom is not harmful to humans.
  • 🎨 Many are beautifully patterned from metallic sheens to bold stripes.
  • 🕸️ They do spin silk to create draglines, retreats, and egg sacs.
  • 💃 Males perform elaborate dances often with leg waves and body vibrations to impress females.
  • 📏 Tiny but bold most range from 1 to 25 mm but are active, curious, and fearless.
  • 🌿 You can often find them on sunny surfaces like garden walls, fences, tree bark, or even inside homes.
  • 🤲 To handle one let it walk onto your hand gently; avoid quick movements and never trap it under a cup. Be careful not to squish them, as they are delicate creatures.
